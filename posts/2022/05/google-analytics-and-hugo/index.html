So you want to add GA4 to your Hugo website?

Here is a step-by-step guide to adding Google Analytics to your Hugo website.

  1. Setup a Google Analytics account

Head over here and make an account (or sign in if you already have one).

Create a property (accessed through admin), add a stream, and point it to your site URL.

Once done, head over to admin > property > Data Streams and go to your newly created stream. Copy the gtag. It should look something like this G-XXXXXXXXXX.

  1. Edit config.toml

In your config.toml file, add a new property under params as follows:

[params]
  ...
  GoogleAnalyticsID = "G-XXXXXXXXXX"
  ...

  1. Edit your layouts folder

Depending on your theme, this step may differ slightly. I have used the hello-friend-ng. I initially tried to add it to the websites layouts folder, but it was throwing a few errors :(.

For this theme, I add a partial with the following script (from the google analytics page).

<!-- Add this in 
themes/hello-friend-ng/layouts/partials/analytics-gtag.html -->
<script async src="https://www.googletagmanager.com/gtag/js?id={{ .Site.params.GoogleAnalyticsID }}"></script>
<script>
  window.dataLayer = window.dataLayer || [];
  function gtag(){dataLayer.push(arguments);}
  gtag('js', new Date());

  gtag('config', '{{ .Site.params.GoogleAnalyticsID }}');
</script>

Then I simply added the partial to the <head/> of the baseof.html.

<!-- Add this in
themes/hello-friend-ng/layouts/_default/ -->
<head>
    {{ partial "head.html" . }}
    {{ if and (hugo.IsProduction) (.Site.Params.googleAnalyticsID) }}
    {{ partial "analytics-gtag.html" . }}
    {{ end }}
</head>

The script makes sure that Google Analytics is only running on your actual website, and not on localhost for testing as well.

  1. All set!

Head to your Google Analytics page and check out reports. If all goes well, congratulations! According to google, you are the first of many users to see this webpage :)
